## Product Overview
**One-liner:** Your AI marketing team, always on.

**What it does:** Maroa.ai is an AI-powered marketing automation platform for small businesses. Owners set a goal; AI agents plan campaigns, write content, run Meta ads, schedule social posts, track competitors, and email customers — autonomously. The owner gets a one-line summary at the end of the day instead of a to-do list.

**Product category:** AI marketing automation / autonomous marketing agents (sits on the same shelf as Buffer, Hootsuite, Mailchimp, Jasper — but positioned against the *category* of "tools that wait for you" rather than any single competitor).

**Product type:** B2B SaaS (self-serve, web app).

**Business model:** Subscription with free tier.
- **Free** — $0
- **Growth** — $49/mo
- **Agency** — $99/mo (multi-workspace, for agencies managing multiple clients)
- 14-day free trial on paid tiers, no credit card required

## Problems & Pain Points
**Core problem:** Marketing tools sell themselves as "easy" but actually require the owner to be the copywriter, designer, strategist, and analyst. Doing it yourself eats hours; hiring an agency costs €800+/mo and you *still* approve every post.

**Why alternatives fall short:**
- **Tools (Buffer, Hootsuite, Later, Mailchimp):** Give you a blank canvas. You still have to come up with ideas, write the copy, design the image, decide when to post, then check if it worked.
- **Agencies:** Expensive, slow, generic. You're paying €800/mo and still on weekly calls approving copy.
- **AI writing tools (Jasper, Copy.ai):** Help with one task (writing) but don't *do* anything — no posting, no ads, no measurement.
- **Doing nothing:** Competitors who do market eat your lunch — one Instagram post, one ad, one email at a time.

**What it costs them:** ~5-10 hours/week trying to do marketing themselves, OR €600-1500/mo for an agency they still have to manage, OR (most common) flat sales because they did nothing.

**Emotional tension:** Guilt ("I know I should be posting"), overwhelm ("where do I even start"), envy/fear ("my competitor's Instagram is way more active than mine"), and a low-grade dread of opening Buffer to a blinking cursor.

## Competitive Landscape
**Direct (autonomous AI marketing):**
- Newer entrants positioning as "AI marketing employee" / "marketing copilot" — most are still in the "AI helps you write" category, not "AI does it for you"
- Falls short because: most are AI-assisted *tools* (you still drive), not AI agents that *act*

**Secondary (do-it-yourself tools):**
- **Buffer / Later / Hootsuite** — scheduling tools. You bring the content.
- **Mailchimp / Klaviyo** — email tools. You bring the content.
- **Meta Ads Manager** — DIY ad platform. You bring the strategy and the patience to learn it.
- Falls short because: they're canvases, not workers. Owner is still doing all the thinking.

**Indirect (delegate to a human):**
- Marketing agencies (€600-2000/mo)
- Freelance social media managers
- Hiring a part-time marketing person
- Falls short because: expensive, slow, requires management overhead, brand voice drifts.

## Differentiation
**Key differentiators:**
- **Agents that act, not assist** — Maroa doesn't draft for your approval (default), it ships. Approval mode is optional.
- **Full-stack, not point-tool** — content + social + ads + email + competitor tracking + analytics in one autonomous loop, not 5 SaaS subscriptions
- **Goal-driven, not task-driven** — owner sets an outcome ("more weekend bookings"), agents figure out the campaigns
- **Built for SMBs, not enterprise** — pricing and onboarding designed for owner-operators, not marketing teams

**How we do it differently:** A central "AI Brain" agent (Claude Opus) sets the strategy weekly. Specialist agents (content, ads, social, email, competitor) execute on a daily/weekly cadence via 47 n8n workflows orchestrated against the owner's business profile.

**Why that's better:** The owner gets the output of a 4-person marketing team for $49/mo, with one daily summary instead of a meeting.

**Why customers choose us:**
- They've tried Buffer/Mailchimp and bounced because "it's still my job"
- They've tried an agency and bounced because of cost or quality
- They want marketing to *happen*, not to *manage* it

## Objections

| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| "AI content sounds generic" | Agents are trained on your brand voice, products, and past posts. You can review the first week before going autopilot. Quality bar is "would you have approved this anyway?" |
| "I don't want to lose control of my brand" | Two modes — Approval (review before publish) or Autopilot (publish, you can edit/delete after). Start with Approval, switch when you trust it. |
| "$49/mo is a lot for a small business" | One agency hour costs more. The free tier covers content generation only — paid tiers add ads + social + email automation. |
| "I've been burned by AI tools that don't work" | 14-day trial, no credit card. If the first week of content doesn't sound like you, it's free. |
| "How is this different from ChatGPT?" | ChatGPT writes when you ask. Maroa writes, posts, runs the ads, measures the result, and adjusts — without being asked. |

**Anti-persona:** Mid-market and enterprise (>50 employees) with a dedicated marketing team. Brands with strict legal/compliance review on every post (regulated industries — healthcare, finance, legal). Anyone whose voice is so unique that no AI can match it (high-end luxury, very personal-brand creators).

## Switching Dynamics
**Push (away from current solution):** "I keep paying for Buffer and not using it." / "My agency contract is up and I can't justify another year." / "I went 3 weeks without posting again."

**Pull (toward Maroa):** "It actually does the work." / "I can sleep on Sunday and still have a content week ready." / "The Meta ads thing alone is worth it."

**Habit (what keeps them stuck):** Existing tool subscriptions, fear of switching mid-campaign, "I'll get to marketing next month."

**Anxiety (about switching to Maroa):** "Will the AI sound like me?" / "What if it posts something embarrassing?" / "How is this not just another tool I'll forget about?"
